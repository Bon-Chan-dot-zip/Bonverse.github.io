Sectors

After the collapse, GaiaNet was greatly affected. This caused errors and instabilites which results in its "fracturing" into multiple disconnected subnets. Two pre-collapse subnets that were affected were the DimNet and TerraNet. DimNet was a subnet responsible for keeping the rest of the subnets from causing any spatial anomalies and rectifying any if they did occur. TerraNet was a corporate owned subnet used for terraforming. After the collpase these two systems went haywire. Leading to planet-wide catastrophies such as earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, spatial implosions, dimensional shifts, etc. In the current day these two subnets have been mostly inactive for many centuries, meaning that consistent and reliable exploration of the planet is now feasible. Because of the inconsistency of linear travel, individual landmasses and continents arent used often to denote regions. Instead, regions are placed under the name of Sectors, and are determined by biomes, local geography, ecology, history, etc. A sector with a number in front of its name is a sector that has been explored but then changed in some way, the number in front denotes the amount of times it has changed.
